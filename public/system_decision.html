Session Zero — Game Selection

Which Sci-Fi TTRPG?

Three games. One table. Read the profiles, check the comparison at the end, and make a call.

All three suit experienced 5e players. None require months of prep to get started. One-shots are recommended to begin with — each game has enough material to fill a solid evening without committing to a campaign.

Dark Matter

Publisher: Mage Hand Press
System: D&D 5e (fully compatible)
Tone: Space opera — flexible

D&D 5e reimagined on a galactic scale. If you know how to play D&D, you already know how to play this. Everything familiar is still here — just set in deep space, with laser rifles, power armour, void jumps, and alien horrors.

The Setting

You are in “the Verse” — a Milky Way where ancient beings called Maws serve as jump gates and dark matter engines shrink ships down to travel through a deadly Void dimension. Magic and technology coexist. Empires span star systems. Earth exists, but it sits in a Dead Magic Zone.

Draws inspiration from Star Wars, The Expanse, Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy, and Doom. If your group has watched Guardians of the Galaxy, Firefly, or Farscape and wanted to play in that universe, this is the closest fit.

What’s Good

  • Zero system relearning — pure 5e under the hood
  • Flexible tone works for different moods: pulpy adventure, cosmic horror, political intrigue
  • Skills adapted for sci-fi contexts: Piloting, Data, Technology
  • Active development — updated edition recently released

Worth Knowing

  • More space fantasy than hard sci-fi — magic and tech coexist, which not everyone enjoys
  • Can feel like D&D with a coat of paint rather than something genuinely different
  • Tone flexibility is a strength, but it means the game lacks a strong default atmosphere

Pick This If…

You want to jump straight in without learning a new system. Dark Matter is the path of least resistance — familiar rules, new universe. Good for a group that’s uncertain about trying something different.

Death in Space

Publisher: Stockholm Kartell / Free League
System: OSR d20 (familiar core roll, simpler than 5e)
Tone: Grim survival horror

The solar system is dying. Resources are running out. Strange cosmic forces corrupt the weak. You are scavengers, salvagers, and survivors clinging to existence in a crumbling industrial hellscape. Nothing is safe. Nothing lasts.

The Setting

The Tenebrous Systems — a corner of space where a dying sun casts everything in twilight. No Earth. No hope of rescue. Factions fight over scraps. Ancient alien technology is dangerous and poorly understood. Void corruption warps those who push too hard.

Think Alien crossed with Dark Souls crossed with Fallout. Deep-space survival with body horror undertones and industrial decay everywhere you look.

What’s Good

  • Distinctive aesthetic — nothing else on this list looks or feels like this
  • Void corruption system generates drama automatically from failed rolls — the dice create tension without the GM having to manufacture it
  • d20 base means the core roll is immediately familiar
  • Short character creation — playing in 15 minutes

Worth Knowing

  • Rules are intentionally sparse — the GM fills gaps with improvisation
  • Characters start weak and stay fragile — very different from 5e heroics
  • Darker tone may not suit every group’s mood for every session

Pick This If…

You want something that feels genuinely different from D&D. The dying solar system aesthetic is unlike anything else on this list. Characters are fragile, decisions matter, and the void corruption system creates drama without you needing to force it.

Cyberpunk Red

Publisher: R. Talsorian Games
System: Custom d10 — roll 1d10 + skill + stat vs a difficulty value
Tone: Gritty street-level cyberpunk noir

Night City, 2045. The Fourth Corporate War has ended and left the city in ruins. Corporations still run everything. The streets are full of edgerunners, fixers, and the desperate. You take jobs, take risks, and try to survive in a world that chews people up and spits them out.

The Setting

Night City is the same city as Cyberpunk 2077 — just earlier, darker, and more broken. Before the neon rebuild. The “Time of the Red” is a period of recovery and power struggles, where the old megacorps are weakened but not gone, and the streets belong to whoever is tough enough to hold them.

Direct line to Altered Carbon, Neuromancer, and Blade Runner. If your group has played Cyberpunk 2077 or watched Cyberpunk: Edgerunners on Netflix, they already know this world intimately which is a significant advantage at the table.

What’s Good

  • Best-written core rulebook of any game on this list — genuinely easy to learn from
  • Free Easy Mode starter set on DriveThruRPG with pregen characters and a pre-written gig — ready to run with minimal prep
  • Official companion app handles most of the number-crunching at the table
  • Rich, detailed setting with decades of lore to draw on if you want it

Worth Knowing

  • More to learn than Death in Space or Dark Matter — rewards a few hours of reading first
  • Combat is deadly and tactical — different feel from 5e’s heroic action economy
  • The full core book is large; the free starter set is the smarter entry point
  • Night City is a specific, detailed setting — less flexible than Dark Matter’s tone dial

Pick This If…

You want the full cyberpunk experience with proper setting depth and GM support. The free starter kit means you can run a one-shot with minimal prep, and if the group enjoys it there’s a rich system and setting to grow into.
